Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as the "Missile Man of India", was one of the most beloved Presidents and visionary scientists India has ever seen. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, his life journey is a beacon of inspiration for millions across the globe.
A brilliant aerospace engineer, Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development. His work with ISRO and DRDO led to the development of indigenous missiles like Agni and Prithvi, earning him his iconic nickname. He also contributed significantly to India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India and served till 2007. Known as the "People’s President", Dr. Kalam broke barriers with his humility, accessibility, and unwavering focus on youth empowerment and national development.
Apart from his scientific and political achievements, Dr. Kalam was a prolific writer and educator. Books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020 continue to motivate generations. He always believed that “Dream is not what you see in sleep, it is the thing which doesn’t let you sleep.”
Dr. Kalam's life was marked by simplicity, vision, and service to the nation. Even in his final moments, he was delivering a lecture to students — a true symbol of his lifelong dedication. His legacy lives on through his teachings, writings, and the countless lives he touched.
